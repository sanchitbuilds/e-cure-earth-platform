Environmental Damage

How E-Waste Harms Our Planet

Improper disposal of electronics releases toxic substances that contaminate our soil, water, and air.

🌫️ Air Pollution

Burning e-waste releases dioxins, furans, and heavy metals into the atmosphere, causing severe respiratory disease in nearby communities.

💧 Water Contamination

Lead, mercury, and cadmium from circuit boards leach into groundwater and rivers, making water unsafe for drinking and agriculture for decades.

🌱 Soil Degradation

Toxic chemicals destroy soil microbiomes, rendering land infertile and entering the food chain through plants and animals.

Human Health

The Health Consequences

Communities near informal e-waste recycling sites face severe health crises.

🫁 Respiratory Problems

Toxic smoke from burning cables and circuit boards causes chronic bronchitis, asthma, and increased rates of lung cancer.

🧠 Neurological Damage

Lead and mercury exposure causes irreversible brain damage, particularly devastating for children in developing regions.

☠️ Toxic Substance Exposure

Over 1,000 toxic substances including brominated flame retardants and polychlorinated biphenyls accumulate in human tissue.
